Bark & Blade Cookie Policy

This Cookie Policy explains how Bark & Blade ("we," "us," or "our") uses cookies and similar technologies on our website, barkandblade.com (the "Website"). By continuing to browse or use our Website, you are agreeing to our use of cookies in accordance with this policy. We are committed to transparency regarding our use of these technologies, which are essential for providing you with a seamless and personalized experience. Cookies help us understand how you interact with our platform, allowing us to enhance functionality, deliver relevant content, and ensure the security of your online journey with Bark & Blade. This policy provides detailed information about the types of cookies we use, their purposes, and how you can manage your preferences. We believe that informed consent is crucial, and this document is designed to empower you with the knowledge needed to make choices about your data.

Cookies are small text files that are placed on your computer or mobile device when you visit a website. They are widely used to make websites work more efficiently, as well as to provide information to the owners of the site. They enable the website to "remember" your actions or preferences over time. This policy is an integral part of our broader Privacy Policy and Terms and Conditions, reflecting our comprehensive approach to data protection. We encourage you to read this policy carefully to understand how cookies contribute to your Bark & Blade experience and how you can exercise control over their use. Our goal is to ensure that your interaction with our platform is not only inspiring for your wood carving endeavors but also secure and respectful of your privacy choices.

1. What are Cookies?

Cookies are small data files that websites place on your computer or mobile device when you visit them. They are widely used to make websites work, or work more efficiently, as well as to provide reporting information. Cookies enable the website to "remember" your actions or preferences over a period of time, so you don't have to keep re-entering them whenever you come back to the site or browse from one page to another. They are essentially a unique identifier for your browser or device, allowing for a more personalized and efficient browsing experience.

2. First-Party Cookies

First-party cookies are those set directly by the Bark & Blade website (barkandblade.com). These cookies are essential for core website functionality and are often used to remember your preferences, maintain your login session, or track items in your shopping cart. Because they are set by the domain you are visiting, they are generally considered more secure and less privacy-invasive than third-party cookies. Our first-party cookies are crucial for ensuring a smooth and personalized user experience as you navigate our platform and interact with its features.

3. Third-Party Cookies

Third-party cookies are set by domains other than the one you are visiting. These are typically used for cross-site tracking, advertising, or embedding content from external services (e.g., YouTube videos, social media buttons). On Bark & Blade, third-party cookies may be set by our analytics providers, advertising partners, or social media platforms. While we vet our third-party partners, their cookie practices are governed by their own privacy policies. We provide mechanisms for you to manage your preferences for these cookies.

4. Session Cookies

Session cookies are temporary cookies that remain on your device only for the duration of your browsing session. They are essential for maintaining your login state as you navigate through different pages of our website, ensuring you don't have to log in repeatedly. Once you close your browser, these cookies are automatically deleted. They do not collect any personal information for tracking or marketing purposes and are crucial for the basic functionality and usability of the Bark & Blade platform.

5. Persistent Cookies

Persistent cookies remain on your device for a longer period, even after you close your browser, or until you manually delete them. These cookies are used to remember your preferences and settings for future visits, such as remembering your login details for convenience or your preferred language. They also enable us to analyze long-term user behavior and personalize content across multiple sessions. You can manage persistent cookies through your browser settings, allowing you to control their retention.

6. Essential/Strictly Necessary Cookies

These cookies are strictly necessary for the Bark & Blade website to function properly. They enable core functionalities such as secure login, shopping cart management, and remembering your cookie consent preferences. Without these cookies, certain parts of the Website may not be accessible or function correctly. Because they are essential for providing the service you request, they are typically set automatically and do not require your explicit consent in most jurisdictions.

7. Performance/Analytics Cookies

Performance or analytics cookies collect information about how visitors use our website, such as which pages are visited most often, the time spent on pages, and if users encounter any error messages. This data is aggregated and anonymized, meaning it does not personally identify you. It helps us understand user behavior, identify areas for improvement, and optimize the website's performance and content. These cookies are crucial for our continuous improvement efforts, ensuring a better user experience.

8. Functionality Cookies

Functionality cookies allow our website to remember choices you make (such as your username, language, or the region you are in) and provide enhanced, more personal features. For instance, remembering your preferred wood species in the explorer or your last viewed tutorial. These cookies can also be used to remember changes you have made to text size, fonts, and other customizable parts of web pages. The information these cookies collect may be anonymized, and they cannot track your browsing activity on other websites.

9. Advertising/Targeting Cookies

Advertising or targeting cookies are used to deliver more relevant advertisements to you and your interests. They are usually placed by advertising networks with our permission. They remember that you have visited a website and this information is shared with other organizations, such as advertisers. These cookies help us measure the effectiveness of our advertising campaigns and personalize the ads you see both on and off Bark & Blade. Your consent is explicitly requested for the use of these cookies.

10. Social Media Cookies

Our website may include social media features, such as "Like" or "Share" buttons, which may set cookies on your device. These cookies are typically set by the social media platforms themselves (e.g., Facebook, Instagram, YouTube, Pinterest) and allow them to track your browsing activity across different sites, potentially building a profile of your interests. This enables personalized content and advertising on those social media platforms. We encourage you to review the privacy policies of the respective social media companies.

24. Web Beacons and Pixel Tags

They help us determine which emails have been opened by recipients and allow us to gauge the effectiveness of certain communications and marketing campaigns.

25. Local Storage (HTML5)

This data remains on your device until you clear your browser's local storage.

Persistent cookies remain until they expire or are deleted. The expiration period for persistent cookies varies, from a few minutes to several years, depending on their purpose. For example, a cookie remembering your login might last for a few days, while an analytics cookie might persist for a year to track returning visitors.
